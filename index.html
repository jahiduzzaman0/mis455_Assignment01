The War of Independence of Bangladesh in 1971 was a significant event in the history of Bangladesh. Prior to 1971, Bangladesh was part of Pakistan and was known as East Pakistan. However, tensions between East and West Pakistan had been growing for many years, fueled by cultural, linguistic, and economic differences.In March 1971, the Bangladesh Liberation War began when East Pakistani students, intellectuals, and political activists took to the streets to protest against the government of West Pakistan. The conflict escalated quickly, and West Pakistan responded with a brutal military crackdown. The Bangladesh Liberation War is estimated to have resulted in the deaths of between 300,000 and 3 million people, the vast majority of whom were civilians.

Bangladesh and India share a long history of cultural, economic, and political connections. The two countries are neighboring nations and are bound by common linguistic and cultural ties. Historically, the region now known as Bangladesh was part of British India until 1947, when India gained independence from Britain and was divided into two countries: India and Pakistan. In 1971, East Pakistan declared independence from West Pakistan and became the independent country of Bangladesh.
